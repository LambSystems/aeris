# Judging Narrative

## Purpose

This document explains how Aeris should be framed to judges.

Its goal is not to restate the architecture. Its goal is to make the project feel:

- clearly useful
- clearly sustainability-focused
- technically serious
- well-scoped
- memorable

Aeris should not be pitched as “an AI that detects objects.”

It should be pitched as:

> **a system that turns environmental monitoring into real-world protection decisions**

That is the strongest framing.

---

## Core Judge Message

### The one thing judges should remember

**Aeris uses CASTNET environmental exposure data plus scene understanding to decide what outdoor resources should be protected first.**

That sentence contains:
- the dataset
- the technical system
- the action layer
- the sustainability angle

If judges remember that, the project landed.

---

## Problem Framing

### The problem is not:
- lack of dashboards
- lack of alerts
- lack of AI chat interfaces

### The problem is:
Environmental monitoring data exists, but it rarely becomes **actionable resource-level decisions** for people managing real outdoor setups.

That means there is a missing last mile between:
- environmental awareness
- and
- resource protection

This matters for:
- gardens
- field stations
- outdoor workspaces
- exposed tools and equipment
- temporary outdoor setups

Without a practical action layer, valuable resources stay exposed longer than they should, degrade faster, and are replaced more often than necessary.

That is the sustainability problem.

---

## Why This Is Sustainability

Aeris should be framed as a sustainability project because it focuses on:

- preserving outdoor resources under environmental stress
- reducing avoidable degradation
- extending the useful life of equipment and materials
- reducing replacement waste
- helping people adapt to pollution-related environmental exposure

This is not a healthcare pitch.
This is not a convenience pitch.
This is not just environmental awareness.

It is sustainability through:
- preservation
- adaptation
- action

The strongest conceptual statement for judges is:

> **Environmental data should not stop at awareness. It should drive action.**

Most environmental tools stop at:
- maps

- dashboards
- historical charts

Most vision tools stop at:
- classification
- detection
- scene labeling

Aeris combines both and adds a decision layer.

That is the novelty.

---

## Technical Novelty Narrative

Do not pitch Aeris as “we used an LLM and some detection.”

Pitch it as a layered system with clear technical roles:

### 1. Fixed Context
CASTNET-derived environmental context tells Aeris what kind of pollution-related stress matters in this location.

### 2. Dynamic Context
Scene understanding tells Aeris what outdoor resources are actually present and how accessible they are.

### 3. Policy Engine
A deterministic ranking engine decides what should be protected first.

### 4. Explanation Layer
The LLM explains the recommendation in natural language.

This framing is strong because it shows:
- data engineering
- computer vision
- policy logic
- controlled use of AI

That reads as a serious engineering system, not a vibes demo.

## Why the Dataset Matters

Judges may wonder whether the dataset is real or just mentioned in passing.

The answer should be explicit:

> CASTNET is not decorative in Aeris. It is the core environmental context that determines what type of resource protection logic should be activated.

That means:
- the app is not just doing scene analysis
- the environmental mode comes from data
- the recommendation logic changes because of that data

This is a very important point in the pitch.

It does not just display environmental conditions.

### 2. It grounds recommendations in the real world
It does not just output generic advice.

### 3. It is sustainability-native

> **Aeris is a pollution-aware scene analyzer for outdoor sustainability. It uses CASTNET environmental exposure data to understand what kind of pollution stress matters in a location, then scans a real outdoor setup and recommends what resources should be protected first. Instead of stopping at environmental monitoring, Aeris turns data into practical protection decisions that help preserve outdoor materials, tools, and plant resources.**

---

## The 60-Second Pitch

> **Environmental monitoring systems are good at telling us when pollution-related risk exists, but they rarely help people decide what to do with the real resources in front of them. Aeris solves that last-mile problem. It uses CASTNET-based environmental exposure data as Fixed Context, then combines it with scene understanding of a real outdoor setup as Dynamic Context. From there, a deterministic policy engine ranks what should be protected first — for example, a seed tray, a battery pack, or exposed tools. The result is a sustainability tool that turns environmental data into practical resource-protection decisions, helping reduce avoidable degradation and extend the life of outdoor assets.**

## How to Answer “Why Is This Sustainability?”

This is the most important judge question to prepare for.

### Best answer
> Sustainability is not just about monitoring the environment. It is also about helping people preserve resources under environmental stress. Aeris uses pollution exposure data to reduce avoidable degradation, protect outdoor assets, and support adaptation through practical action.

### Shorter answer
> It is sustainability because it helps preserve real resources and reduce replacement waste under environmental stress.

---

## How to Answer “Why Use CASTNET?”

### Best answer
> CASTNET gives us the environmental exposure profile that tells the system what kind of pollution-related stress matters in a location. That context changes what Aeris prioritizes in the scene, so the dataset is part of the decision logic, not just background information.

### Shorter answer
> CASTNET is the fixed environmental context that drives the protection mode.

---

## How to Answer “Why Not Just a Dashboard?”

### Best answer
> Dashboards tell you that risk exists. Aeris tells you what to protect first in the real world. The value is in the action layer, not just the awareness layer.

This answer is very strong.

---

## How to Answer “Why Not Just Object Detection?”

### Best answer
> Object detection alone only tells you what is there. Aeris combines what is there with environmental context and a protection policy to decide what matters first.

---

## How to Answer “Why the LLM?”

### Best answer
> The LLM is only used to explain decisions in natural language. The actual ranking is deterministic and based on structured context.

This reassures judges that the project is not just handing control to a black box.

## Strongest Differentiators

When comparing Aeris to likely hackathon competitors, emphasize these points:

Not fake environmental context, but a real environmental dataset driving behavior.

### 2. Grounded scene intelligence

### 3. Action recommendation

## What Not to Say

Avoid these weaker framings:

- “It’s like Jarvis”
- “It’s a general AI assistant”
- “It’s just object detection for outdoor scenes”
- “It’s kind of sustainability because bad air affects people”
- “It could be used for many things”

These dilute the project.

Stay specific.
