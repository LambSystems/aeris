# Hackathon Context

## Event

**HackAugie 2026** is Augustana College’s first 24-hour hackathon, bringing together students from across the Midwest to build bold, creative, and impactful tech projects.

### Core event details
- **Deadline:** April 19, 2026 at 10:30 AM CDT
- **Location:** Augustana College — Hanson Building
- **Team size:** 2 to 4 members
- **Eligibility:** Ages 18+, US only, invite-only application
- **Current constraint:** approximately **17 hours remaining** at the time of planning

HackAugie is described as:
- beginner friendly
- social good oriented
- multidisciplinary
- open to developers, designers, product thinkers, data enthusiasts, hardware hackers, and storytellers

This context matters because the winning project should not just be technically interesting — it must also be:
- understandable in a short pitch
- visually demoable
- clearly tied to a real problem
- feasible within the remaining time

## Judging Criteria

HackAugie judges projects using the following weighted criteria:

### 1. Completion — **30%**
Does the project work and meet the goals it set out to achieve?

### 2. Problem Solving — **25%**
Does it address a real, relevant problem effectively?

### 3. Originality — **20%**
How creative and unique is the idea?

### 4. Design & User Experience — **15%**
How intuitive and user-friendly is the interface or interaction?

### 5. Technical Complexity — **10%**
How technically impressive is the implementation?

### Strategic implication
This weighting strongly favors:
- shipping a working product
- solving a clear real problem
- telling a coherent story

It does **not** favor overengineering for its own sake.

That means Aeris should optimize for:
- one strong end-to-end demo
- one clear problem statement
- one polished interaction
- visible use of real data
- enough technical depth to feel impressive, but not so much that stability suffers

## Required Dataset Constraint

For the Data Insight Challenge, the team must use at least one provided dataset.

The relevant provided dataset for our project is:

## **Sustainability: Clean Air Status and Trends Network (CASTNET)**

### Dataset summary
The challenge description says this dataset provides air quality data in rural locations, often upwind or downwind of urban areas, including:

- weekly concentrations of sulfur and nitrogen species
- base cations and chloride
- hourly ozone levels
- additional pollutants at some sites
- long-term records spanning 30+ years

### Why this matters for Aeris
This dataset is not being used as a side note.

It is central to the product because Aeris needs a real environmental context layer that answers:

- what kind of pollution-related stress matters here?
- what outdoor resources are most vulnerable under that context?
- what sustainability logic should be activated in the scene?

That is why CASTNET is part of the **core product architecture**, not just part of the slide deck.
